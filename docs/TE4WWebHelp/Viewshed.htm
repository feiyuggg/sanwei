Viewshed

The 3D viewshed calculates and marks all areas on the terrain and on 3D models and objects that are visible from a selected point on the terrain. The defined viewshed is initially displayed in Preview mode. After setting viewshed parameters and repositioning the viewshed as necessary, a high quality viewshed can be calculated and displayed.

To create/edit a viewshed:

1.     On the sidebar, click the Analysis tab > Viewshed.

2.     In the 3D Window, click to define your viewpoint for the 3D viewshed, and then click again to define your desired endpoint (defining the distance of sight). The viewshed is saved and is available from the Objects section of the Analysis tab.

Note:        If editing an existing viewshed, on the Analysis tab, click the viewshed you want to edit, and then click Edit .

3.     The defined viewshed is initially displayed in Preview mode. Generally, you will want to set viewshed parameters (step 4) and reposition the viewshed as necessary, before calculating a high quality viewshed. See step 6 below. The viewshed appears on the terrain as a sector of a circle, where the center point is the viewpoint and the arc is the endpoint (marking the end of the distance of sight analysis). The 3D viewshed is saved and is available from the Objects section of the sidebar’s Analysis tab. Within the sector, areas that are visible from the viewpoint are colored green, whereas areas that are not visible from the viewpoint are colored red.

4.     Click Viewshed Properties  to set its properties:

Object Parameter

Activity

Name

The description or name of the 3D viewshed (read-only).

This text appears in the sidebar’s Analysis tab as the name of the object.

Horizontal FOV

The horizontal angle limits, in degrees, of the viewshed.

Vertical FOV

The vertical angle limits, in degrees, of the viewshed.

Distance

The length of the viewshed analysis from the viewer in meters.

X

The X-coordinate of the viewer location.

Y

The Y-coordinate of the viewer location.

Altitude

The altitude above the ground for the viewer location.

Altitude Method

The altitude method to be used for the viewer location:

§  Absolute – Places the viewer location point at the specified altitude above the terrain database vertical datum base ellipsoid.

§  Relative to Terrain – Places the viewer location point at the specified altitude above the ground.

§  Relative to 3DML –  Places the viewer location point at the specified altitude above the 3DML.

 

5.     If you want to reposition the viewshed, click Move Horizontally  or Move Vertically .

6.     If you want to calculate a high quality viewshed, click Calculate . After all viewshed data is downloaded from the server and calculations are complete, the viewshed is redisplayed with a high degree of precision. The intensity of the viewshed colors is increased to indicate that you are in Calculated mode, and the Calculate button appears dimmed. Note that you can recalculate at any point.

Note:        If changes are made to a viewshed (e.g. properties are modified, or its position is adjusted), the viewshed switches back to Preview mode.

7.     If you want to delete the viewshed, click Delete .
