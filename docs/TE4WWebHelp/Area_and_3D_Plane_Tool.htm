Area and 3D Plane Tool

The area and 3D plane tool measures any of the following:

§  Area on terrain:

§  Area of the horizontal projection (2D measurement) - The area measured is the horizontal projection of the area you have outlined, even if some or all of your selected area encompasses mountainous terrain.

§  Surface area (3D measurement) - The area measured takes into account terrain contours.

§  Area on arbitrary plane

To measure area:

1.     On the sidebar, click the Analysis tab > Area.

2.     For area on terrain, click Horizontal Mode . For area on an arbitrary plane, click 3D Plane Mode .

3.     Click any point on the terrain/plane to define the start point of the measurement. Click to set the second point and then again for each additional point. Right-click or click the check box  to finish the measurement. Before right-clicking, you can click Delete Point  to delete the last point. If the boundary line of the measured area crosses itself, it turns red. The area polygon is filled in yellow, and the area is saved and is available from the Objects section of the sidebar’s Analysis tab. The Area dialog box displays area, total horizontal area, and perimeter.

4.     If the region measured was entirely on the terrain, you can click Terrain Surface  to calculate the surface area of the selected region taking into account terrain contours. The area polygon is overlayed with a white grid representing the terrain surface area, and the total surface area is added to the measurements in the Area – Horizontal Mode dialog box. This option is only available when measuring area on terrain.

5.     If you want to delete the measurement polygon, click Delete
